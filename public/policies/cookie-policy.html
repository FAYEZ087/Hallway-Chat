1. WHAT ARE COOKIES?

Cookies are small text files that a website stores on your device when you visit it. They help the site remember information about your visit — such as whether you are logged in — so you do not have to re-enter details every time you return.

Cookies are not programs and cannot carry viruses or access files on your device. They simply store small pieces of data that help the website work properly.

2. HOW WE USE COOKIES

Hallway uses cookies and similar technologies to:

  • Keep you logged in across sessions so you do not need to re-enter your email every time you visit
  • Remember your session and profile state (e.g., whether your profile setup is complete)
  • Enable core platform functionality like matching and real-time communication
  • Improve platform performance and diagnose technical issues

We do not use cookies for advertising, retargeting, or tracking you across other websites.

4. LOCAL STORAGE & SESSION STORAGE

In addition to cookies, Hallway uses browser local storage and session storage to maintain your login state. Specifically:

  • Supabase session tokens are stored in local storage so that you remain logged in when you close and reopen the browser. This is how persistent login works on Hallway.
  • This data stays on your device and is not transmitted to anyone except Supabase's servers for authentication purposes.

You can clear this data at any time by clearing your browser's site data or logging out of your Hallway account.

5. THIRD-PARTY COOKIES

Some of the services we use may set their own cookies on your device:

  • Supabase — sets session cookies for authentication purposes
  • Vercel Analytics — may set anonymized analytics cookies to measure page performance

These third parties have their own privacy and cookie policies. We encourage you to review them:

  • Supabase Privacy Policy
  • Vercel Privacy Policy

We do not use Google Analytics, Facebook Pixel, or any advertising network cookies.

6. HOW TO CONTROL COOKIES

You have the right to accept or decline cookies. Most browsers automatically accept cookies, but you can modify your browser settings to decline them if you prefer.

Please note: If you disable essential cookies or clear local storage, you will be logged out of Hallway and will need to verify your email again on your next visit. The platform may not function correctly without these.

Here is how to manage cookies in popular browsers:

  • Google Chrome
  • Mozilla Firefox
  • Safari
  • Microsoft Edge

To clear local storage specifically for Hallway, go to your browser's Developer Tools → Application → Local Storage → hallwaychat.online → Clear.
